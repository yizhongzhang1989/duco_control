Use the dropdown to select a controller; click Engage to activate it (only allowed while idle). Force = pure free-drive (target_wrench=0); Motion = pose-hold (snapshots current pose, rigid drive to target); Compliance = pose-hold spring (snapshots current pose at engage and yields to wrench — push the end-effector and it bounces back like a spring).

Controller commands kind: --

The force controller consumes target_wrench and minimises (target - measured). The orchestrator publishes target_wrench=0 at 10 Hz on /cartesian_force_controller/target_wrench, so engage gives you pure free-drive (hand-guidance) -- push the arm and it yields. No per-press commands are needed; just engage and guide.
Motion controller: snapshots current pose on engage, then rigidly drives the end-effector to whatever target you publish. Each jog press computes new_target = current_EE_pose + delta in base_link and publishes it on /cartesian_motion_controller/target_frame. Snap resets the target to the current pose.
Compliance controller (spring-hold): on engage the controller snapshots the current end-effector pose as its target. With target_wrench=0 (published by the orchestrator) and the configured stiffness, pushing the end-effector makes it yield; release and it springs back to the held pose. Use Re-snap hold pose after drift to re-anchor the spring at the current pose. Tune the stiffness.* rows in “Controller parameters” (right panel) to change how stiff the spring is — higher = stiffer. Jog presses move the held pose by an incremental delta.
